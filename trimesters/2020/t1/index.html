2020 Trimester One:
Jan -> April 30th

By Santa Clara Gives,
05/02/2020 10:29pm
In January, we were already worried about the virus that would eventually become the COVID-19 pandemic. We saw that there were a lot of stress surrounding the continuous spreading nature of this virus.

In February, we saw events being canceled. Worse of all, we saw our local businesses begin to lose businesses. Many members of our team had their workplace close down and we began to see a surge in purchases of masks, hand sanitizers, and other forms of PPE.
At this point, we knew we needed to do something. We shouldn't just sit around for it to boil over. What if COVID cases never kept surging?
That's when our core team got together. We didn't know what we were going to do yet but we watched developments carefully and began reaching out to our connections, to be prepared for the worse case. We already started work on this website (albeit under different name).

In March, sadly the number of cases kept going up. At this point, we've begun work on the payment portal and have started researching places where the virus is causing pain and impact.
At this point, applying for a 501(c)(3) status for SantaClaraGives would've taken far too long. That's when our good friends at the nonprofit, BayEcho stepped in. They had the ability to collect tax-deductible donations and allowed us to reach just a little bit further.

Visit BayEcho.org!


In April, we finally began receiving our batch mask orders and are currently looking at possible recipients.
Please visit this spreadsheet to find out where the donations went!

We hope you join us in the fight against COVID-19, so we can help those that need help the most. Let’s do this together.
